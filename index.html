The Three Levels

Recovery Advocacy Framework

Advocacy isn't just about big public statements - it's about reclaiming your voice at every level.

Level 1: Internal Advocacy (Foundation)

  • Challenging ED thoughts: "That's not my value, that's my illness"
  • Self-validation: "My needs are legitimate"
  • Internal permission-giving: "I'm allowed to take up space"

Real talk: Your ED wants you voiceless. Speaking internally is rebellion.

Level 2: Interpersonal Advocacy (Building)

  • Setting meal support boundaries: "Please don't comment on my food"
  • Medical advocacy: "That weight-focused approach doesn't work for me"
  • Social navigation: "I'm not discussing diets"
  • Asking for needs: "I need flexibility with my schedule for PHP"

Real talk: People might be uncomfortable. Their discomfort isn't your responsibility.

Level 3: Community/Systems Advocacy (Peak)

  • Challenging insurance denials
  • Educating others about ED realities
  • Workplace mental health advocacy
  • Breaking stigma in your communities

Real talk: You don't have to be fully recovered to advocate. Sometimes advocating IS recovery.

Advocacy in Action

Practice Scenarios

Let's practice advocacy at different levels. Select scenarios that resonate with your current challenges:

High Stakes

Coverage Denial

"Your insurance denies PHP coverage saying you're 'weight restored' and therefore recovered, ignoring your team's recommendations."

üí° Tip: Use clinical language from your team. Document specific behaviors and symptoms.
Phone Advocacy

Peer Reviewer Pushback

"The insurance peer reviewer interrupts you and says 'But you're eating now, right? So you're fine.'"

üí° Tip: "Eating doesn't equal recovered. Let me explain the medical necessity..."
Restaurant Challenge

Server Commentary

"The server says 'Wow, good for you for being so healthy!' when you order, triggering your ED voice."

üí° Tip: You don't owe anyone an explanation. A simple "thanks" and redirect is enough.
Family Dinner

Meal Plan Timing

"Your family wants to eat dinner at 8pm, but your meal plan requires you to eat by 6pm for evening snack timing."

üí° Tip: "My recovery requires specific timing. I need to eat at 6."
Workplace Wellness

Diet Culture at Work

"Your workplace starts a 'Biggest Loser' competition and colleagues pressure you to join the 'fun.'"

üí° Tip: "I'm focusing on health in different ways" or "That doesn't align with my values."
Friend Group

Before/After Photos

"Friends are sharing old photos and say 'You looked so good here!' pointing to a photo from your sickest point."

üí° Tip: "I was actually really sick then. I'm healthier now." Keep it simple.
Medical Team

New Provider Education

"A new psychiatrist says 'You don't look like you have an eating disorder' in your intake."

üí° Tip: "Eating disorders don't have a look. Here are my symptoms and needs..."
Team Conflict

Conflicting Recommendations

"Your therapist and dietitian give conflicting advice about exercise. You're stuck in the middle."

üí° Tip: "I need my team aligned. Can we have a team meeting?"
